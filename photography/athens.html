Ugur Kostak

Athens

Published: January 2026

Athens is rarely just one thing at once. The Acropolis is visible from almost any rooftop, but the texture of the city lives in the streets below—graffiti, cafés, cats asleep on warm stone, neighborhoods quietly shifting from one decade to another.

This series wanders away from the monuments to the everyday: a bakery in Exarcheia at first light, the long flat shadows of Plaka, fruit stalls in Monastiraki. The aim was to photograph Athens as it is lived, not as it is remembered.

The Mediterranean winter light is forgiving and slow, and made for long handheld exposures of interior cafés and the occasional rainy alley.
