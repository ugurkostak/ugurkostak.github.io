Ugur Kostak

Istanbul

Published: December 2025

Istanbul does not really sit in one place—it spills over hills, across the Bosphorus, between continents and centuries. This series follows the city's seams: ferries, bridges, the call to prayer drifting between minarets, the constant motion of a place that refuses to settle.

Mornings on the Galata bridge, fishermen line the rails while commuters stream past below. In the afternoon, the light slides across the Hagia Sophia and the Blue Mosque from opposite sides, and the rooftops of Sultanahmet turn a slow ochre.

The photographs were made over several short trips. Istanbul rewards return visits more than checklists—each season changes the color and pace of the streets.
