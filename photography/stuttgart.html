Ugur Kostak

Stuttgart

Published: March 2026

Stuttgart's urban landscape sits in a basin ringed by vineyards and forested hills, a geography that gives the city an unusually layered visual rhythm. These photographs trace the contrast between its post-war architecture and the older fragments that survived.

Walking the city from the Schlossplatz outward, one notices how Stuttgart layers eras side by side: a modernist façade next to a sandstone arch, a tram gliding past a sculpture from a different century. The series tries to capture that quiet juxtaposition without forcing a narrative.

Most frames were shot in late afternoon, when the basin's light softens and the city's stone surfaces take on a warmer cast. Equipment was kept minimal—one body, one prime lens—to stay responsive to the street.
