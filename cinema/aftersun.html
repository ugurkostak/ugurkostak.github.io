Ugur Kostak

Aftersun

Published: April 2022

Charlotte Wells' directorial debut "Aftersun" is a tender and emotionally complex exploration of father-daughter relationships and the passage of time. The film follows a young girl on a holiday with her father at a Turkish resort, capturing both the joy of the moment and the underlying sadness of their situation.

Paul Mescal delivers a nuanced performance as the father, conveying vulnerability and love without words. The cinematography is intimate and warm, making the viewer feel present in every golden-hour moment. Wells creates a film that feels both specific and universal—many viewers will find themselves reflected in the complex dynamics between parent and child.

The film's structure, moving between past and present, adds layers of meaning to the narrative. "Aftersun" is a quiet, contemplative film that lingers with the viewer long after the credits roll, inviting reflection on family, memory, and the inevitable passage of time.
