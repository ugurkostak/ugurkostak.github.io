Ugur Kostak

Everything Everywhere All at Once

Published: March 2022

Daniel Kwan and Daniel Scheinert's "Everything Everywhere All at Once" is a dazzling, mind-bending journey through multiple universes. This multiverse epic combines jaw-dropping visual effects with a deeply human story about a Chinese-American woman grappling with family, identity, and her place in an infinite cosmos.

Michelle Yeoh delivers a powerhouse performance, anchoring the film's chaotic energy with emotional authenticity. The film's visual style is inventive and exhilarating, employing diverse cinematic techniques across different universes. Yet beneath all the multiverse chaos lies a tender meditation on acceptance, love, and human connection.

The supporting performances from Ke Huy Quan and Stephanie Hsu are excellent, and the film's rapid-fire humor balances its more philosophical moments perfectly. "Everything Everywhere All at Once" is a rare blockbuster that combines spectacle with substance, proving that ambitious, boundary-pushing filmmaking can find mainstream success.
